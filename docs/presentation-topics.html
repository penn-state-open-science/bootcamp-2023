Presentation topics

The following are potential presentation topics we are considering for Day 1 and Day 2.

These are also topics that might be offered later on after the bootcamp.

Some of the presentations have been suggsted by a specific person, and others are topics that Rick Gilmore thought might be appropriate for a person or group. The person indicated in the parentheses is the tentative or proposed coordinator for that workshop as of 2023-04-10. Question marks (?) indicate that the person has not yet confirmed their interest or availability. The information is merely for planning purposes.

What is open science? Why do open science?

These are some possible presentations for Day 1.

Keynote (TBD)

Case study: Reproducibility in neuroimaging (Frank Hillary? & Nicole Lazar?)

Case study: Big data behavioral science: The Play & Learning Across a Year (PLAY) Project (Rick Gilmore)

Reproducible research in economics (Lars Vilhuber, Cornell?)

Reproducible and robust research has wider reach (?)

Types of reproducibility. How building reproducible research workflows can make your work more robust and more readily shareable.

How to do open science?

These are some possible presentations for Day 2.

Preregistration & registered reports (Bruce Desmarais?)

What is preregistration? What are preregistered reports? When and why would you want to use these tools?

Replication (Kevin McManus)

Why are replication studies important? How to plan, conduct, and publish replication studies.

Responsible and robust research (?)

What are questionable research practices? Why are they questionable? How do we avoid them.

Literate Programming with R Markdown and Quarto (Rick Gilmore)

What is literate programming? How can literate programming tools improve your research productivity? This workshop will demonstrate the many uses of R Markdown and its successor, Quarto in research, teaching, and service. These tools can be used to create slides, papers, and web sites using the same source materials.

Literate programming with Jupyter notebooks (Mike Burnham?)

Version control with git, GitHub, and GitLab (Alaina Pearce?)

Open access publishing (Briana Wham?)

Where and how to share your research so that the maximum number of people can read it.

Sharing data and materials (Rick Gilmore & Bruce Desmarais?)

Where can you share data and materials? This workshop will demonstrate how researchers can use the Open Science Framework (OSF), Databrary, and other data and materials repositories both to share data and materials and to repurpose data and materials shared by others.

Good enough practices for data and project management (?)

Best practices can be hard to implement, so what open science practices are both ueseful and “good enough” Wilson et al. (2017) to have an impact on your work?

Data management (Briana Wham?)

How to manage data to meet funder requirements and make sharing with collaborators and other researchers less burdensome.

Where to start? Open science for early career researchers (Daisy Lei? & Hollie Mullin?)

Where can early career researchers start their open science journey?
