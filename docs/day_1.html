Day 1 (Wed, Aug 9)

Location: 102 Marriott Foundation Building

All times are Eastern Daylight Time (EDT).

Morning

Themes: What is open science? Why do open science? Open Science at Penn State.

08:30 am • Coffee

09:00 am • Welcome. Frank Hillary & Briana Wham

09:15 am • What is open science? Why open science?, Rick Gilmore

10:15 am • Break

10:45 am • Open science @ Penn State

This session will feature a series of short (3-5 min) “blitz” talks about initiatives in open science that are going on at Penn State.

  • Case study: Reproducibility in neuroimaging (Frank Hillary)
  • Case study: AI replication markets for the DARPA SCORE initiative (Sarah Rajtmajer)
  • Case study: Open Access at Penn State (Briana Wham)
  • Case study: Data Commons (Maurie Kelly)
  • Case study: ScholarSphere (Briana Wham)
  • Case study: Using the Open Science Framework (OSF) (Alaina Pearce)
  • Case study: Penn State’s subscription to the Qualitative Data Repository (QDR) (Briana Wham)
  • Case study: Databrary & The PLAY Project (Rick Gilmore) | HTML slides.

11:45 am • Lunch break

Afternoon

Theme: Getting started with open science

01:15 pm • Keynote: Replication of preclinical cancer biology: Challenges or opportunities?, Tim Errington, Senior Director of Research, Center for Open Science

02:15 pm • Break

02:45 pm • Good enough practices for data and project management, Alaina Pearce

03:45 pm • Day 1 wrap-up

04:15 pm • End of Day 1
